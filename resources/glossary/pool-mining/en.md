---
term: POOL (Mining)
---

Refers to a set of miners who collaborate by combining their computing power to participate in the search for valid proofs of work on Bitcoin. This pooling into a single organization is a solution to the increasing difficulty of bitcoin mining, which makes it too unlikely for an individual miner to compete and earn rewards on a stable basis. Miners within a mining pool contribute with their machines to the search for valid shares. When a block is mined by the pool, the reward - comprising the newly created bitcoins as well as the transaction fees included in the block - is distributed among the pool members according to the chosen remuneration method. This distribution is proportional to the computing power each miner has contributed.

By joining forces, miners in a pool increase their chances of finding blocks and thus reduce their variance. This ensures a more regular and predictable source of income than solo mining, where a miner may not earn rewards for long periods. This allows them to smooth out their earnings, giving them greater visibility over their activity, particularly when it comes to meeting the various costs involved.
