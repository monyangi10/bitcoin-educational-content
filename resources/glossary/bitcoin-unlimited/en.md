---
term: BITCOIN UNLIMITED
---

Proposal to update the Bitcoin protocol that appeared in late summer 2016, aiming to increase block size flexibly via a hard fork.
Supported by the big blockers during the Blocksize War, notably Roger Ver, this approach differed from Bitcoin Classic by not setting a strict upper limit on block size. Instead, it allowed users to configure their own parameters: maximum generation size, additional block size, and acceptance depth. 
Despite this flexibility, Bitcoin Unlimited faced criticism for its technical complexity and several security vulnerabilities.
This proposal was ultimately never adopted.
