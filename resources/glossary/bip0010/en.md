---
term: BIP0010
definition: Initial proposal for multisignature transactions, withdrawn and replaced by BIP11 and BIP16.
---

The BIP10, proposed by Gavin Andresen in 2011, aimed to introduce the concept of multisignature transactions. Its primary goal was to standardize how payments could be conditioned on multiple signatures (multisig) by introducing a new type of script. 
The proposal suggested allowing transaction creators to specify multiple public keys and a rule requiring a defined number of those keys to sign in order to validate the transaction. 
However, BIP10 was withdrawn and never integrated into the protocol. The functionalities it envisioned were addressed by other subsequent BIPs, such as BIP11 and BIP16, which introduced more sophisticated and flexible multisig mechanisms for Bitcoin.
