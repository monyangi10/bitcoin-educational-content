---
term: BIP0002
---

A document authored by Luke Dashjr in June 2012 that established specific criteria for the acceptance of future Bitcoin Improvement Proposals (BIPs). BIP2 introduced the concepts of BIPs being "accepted", "rejected", or "withdrawn", and specified the conditions required for each status. 
A BIP is considered "accepted" if it receives significant support from the Bitcoin community, including developers and users. A BIP is "rejected" when it does not gain enough support or presents insurmountable problems. Finally, a BIP can be "withdrawn" by its author at any time. BIP2 also more precisely redefines the methodology and format introduced with BIP1.
