---
term: 51 PERCENT ATTACK
---

A hypothetical scenario in the Bitcoin system in which a malicious actor controls more than 50% of the total mining computational power (hashrate). With such dominance, the attacker can manipulate the consensus process, enabling malicious actions such as double spending, where the same bitcoins are spent on a chain that later becomes obsolete, and again on the valid chain. Another potential outcome of a 51% attack is transaction censorship. However, executing a 51% attack requires substantial financial, human, energy, and technical resources, making it highly likely that the malicious actor would be detected  before the attack can occur. Although theoretically possible, a 51% attack on Bitcoin is considered highly unlikely due to the decentralization of mining and the enormous computational power currently deployed.

> ► *This attack is also known as the “Goldfinger attack”.*
