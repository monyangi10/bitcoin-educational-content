---
term: 51% attack
definition: An attack where a malicious actor controls more than half of the mining hash power, allowing them to manipulate transactions, notably by performing double spends.
---

Hypothetical scenario on the Bitcoin system in which a malicious actor controls more than 50% of the total mining computing power (hashrate). With such dominance, the attacker can manipulate the consensus process, enabling malicious actions such as double spending, where the same bitcoins are spent first on a chain that ultimately becomes obsolete, and then a second time on the valid chain. Another objective of a 51% attack is transaction censorship. However, carrying out a 51% attack requires considerable financial, human, energy, and technical resources, and makes the malicious actor likely to be discovered before the attack takes place. Although theoretically possible, a 51% attack on Bitcoin is considered very unlikely due to the decentralization of mining and the large amount of computing power currently deployed. This attack is also referred to as the "Goldfinger Attack".
