---
term: BETTERHASH
---

Mining protocol developed by Matt Corallo in 2018 in an attempt to counter the growing centralization of mining on pools. It differs from Stratum, which was the standard at the time, by offering hashers more control over the selection of transactions to be included in block templates. The main idea behind BetterHash is to give chasers back the possibility of managing block template construction themselves, while retaining the advantages of mining pools such as reduced revenue variance.

In the Stratum protocol, the mining pools control the creation of block templates, i.e. they choose which transactions are included in the blocks and also determine the chain on which Miner. This centralization weakens Bitcoin, as it makes the transaction confirmation process vulnerable to censorship.

BetterHash allows choppers to take back control of these operations, while letting the pool manage the distribution of rewards. In a way, it's one of the precursors of StratumV2.
