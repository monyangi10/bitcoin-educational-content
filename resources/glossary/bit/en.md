---
term: Bit
definition: Fundamental unit of digital information that can take two values 0 or 1.
---

The word “bit” is a contraction of “binary” and “digit.” In computing and cryptology, a bit is the fundamental unit of digital information and represents the smallest possible quantity of information. A bit can only take two distinct values: `0` or `1`. These values, known as binary states, can represent various concepts such as `yes` or `no`, `true` or `false`, and `on` or `off`. Bits form the foundation of digital systems and are used to store and transmit information in computers and networks. The name “Bitcoin” likely comes from combining “Bit”, referring to the electronic nature of the payment system, and “Coin”, referring to its monetary function.
