---
term: ASIC
definition: A specialized integrated circuit designed to compute SHA256 hashes for Bitcoin mining, offering energy efficiency far superior to CPU and GPU.
---

An ASIC is an electronic component designed to perform a specific function with optimal efficiency. In the context of Bitcoin mining, ASICs are specialized integrated circuits that perform hashing operations at high speed and with low energy consumption. They are specialized in executing the `SHA256` hashing function used in the proof-of-work mechanism. ASIC originally refers to the name of the chip. By extension, the acronym "ASIC" is often also used to refer to the machine that hosts this chip. Thus, computers specialized in Bitcoin mining are sometimes called "ASICs" or "miners". ASICs have gradually replaced other mining methods, such as the use of processors (CPU) in the early days of Bitcoin and later graphics cards (GPU), due to their superior energy efficiency and much higher hash rate.

The acronym "*ASIC*" stands for "*Application-Specific Integrated Circuit*".
