---
term: DEMEURAGE
---

Cost associated with holding a currency. By extension, demeurage sometimes designates a monetary mechanism in which prolonged ownership of a currency results in a penalty for its owner, for example, by gradually reducing its value. The aim is often to improve the speed of circulation of money by discouraging long-term savings.

This mechanism is sometimes proposed for implementation on Bitcoin with the aim of supporting the transaction fee market and distributing system costs more equitably. Indeed, the cost of running Bitcoin will eventually fall solely on those who carry out transactions, and not on the savers who benefit from the system's security. However, the Bitcoin community widely rejects the idea of residurage, as it penalizes long-term holders and is not in line with BTC's store-of-value characteristic. Since it imposes a progressive loss of value, it can be perceived as an infringement of the right to own.

One could argue against residurage by explaining that the cost of the current system already inevitably falls on savers, given that they had to carry out a transaction at some point to obtain their bitcoins. What's more, the security of a given UTXO increases greatly with the first few blocks after its creation, but over time, the additional security obtained with each block becomes increasingly marginal. So it's not necessarily unfair to shift the cost of mining onto long-term holders.

In particular, demeurage was applied to Altcoin Freicoin, a Fork from Bitcoin that implemented a negative interest rate system.
