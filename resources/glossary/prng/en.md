---
term: GNPA
---

Short for "Pseudo-random number generator". GNPAs are a class of algorithms used to generate sequences of approximately random numbers, starting from an initial state called a seed (seed). In cryptography, GNPA is used to generate keys, initialization vectors and other elements requiring randomness. A good GNPA must have properties such as output uniformity, unpredictability and resistance to predictive attacks. Unlike true random number generators, GNPAs are deterministic and reproducible. On Bitcoin, GNPAs can be used on portfolio management software or hardware wallets, to generate the recovery phrase that underpins deterministic and hierarchical portfolios.
