---
term: CONDENSAT
---

In cryptography, means the result (or output) produced by applying a cryptographic hash function to a set of data. The condensate is a string of characters of fixed size, generally represented as a series of numbers and letters in hexadecimal notation (base 16). This result has the particularity of being almost unique and specific to the input data, so that a minimal change in the input will produce a completely different condensate. Cryptographic hash functions are designed to be unidirectional and collision-resistant, making it very difficult to recover the original data from the condensate, or to find two distinct inputs producing the same condensate.
