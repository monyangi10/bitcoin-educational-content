---
term: BITCOIN XT
---

Fork of Bitcoin launched in 2015 by well-known developer Mike Hearn and supported by Gavin Andresen (former lead maintainer of Bitcoin after Satoshi’s departure).
Initially, Bitcoin XT was a compatible implementation of the Bitcoin Core protocol. 
However, in August 2015, version 0.11A of Bitcoin XT adopted BIP101: a hard fork proposal to increase block size from 1 MB to 8 MB, with a planned doubling every two years until reaching just over 8 GB per block by 2036.
This proposal was one of the triggers of the Blocksize War that took place between 2015 and 2017. It had support from a significant share of miners and from influential companies such as BitPay, Blockchain.info, and Circle. 
Ultimately, Bitcoin XT failed to gather enough community support. Mike Hearn eventually announce his departure from the community and the sale of his bitcoins, and expressed his disappointment in a blog post, notably stating that “Bitcoin has failed.”
