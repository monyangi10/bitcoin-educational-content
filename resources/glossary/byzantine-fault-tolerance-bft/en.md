---
term: BYZANTINE FAULT TOLERANCE (BFT)
---

A concept used in distributed systems to ensure reliable and secure consensus, even in the presence of faulty or malicious nodes.

This concept is related to the [Byzantine Generals Problem](https://planb.academy/resources/glossary/byzantine-generals-problem), a metaphor illustrating the challenges of coordination and decision-making in a distributed network where actors may not trust each other.

> ► *The Proof-of-Work (PoW) algorithm used by Bitcoin is a consensus mechanism that achieves "Byzantine Fault Tolerance (BFT)." For more information, see the definition of **[BYZANTINE GENERALS PROBLEM](https://planb.academy/it/resources/glossary/byzantine-generals-problem)**.*
