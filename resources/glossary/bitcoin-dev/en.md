---
term: Bitcoin-dev
definition: Email mailing list dedicated to technical discussions about the development of the Bitcoin protocol.
---

E-mail mailing list dedicated to the development of the Bitcoin protocol. Bitcoin-dev was created by Jeff Garzik in 2011 and initially hosted on Sourceforge.net. The mailing list was then moved to Linux Foundation, then to Open Source Lab, and finally, since February 2024, it has been hosted on Google Groups: https://groups.google.com/g/bitcoindev

This mailing list serves as a discussion hub for developers and researchers focused on Bitcoin’s evolution. Discussions are strictly technical or academic. Draft BIPs (Bitcoin Improvement Proposals) are usually shared here before potentially being integrated into the GitHub repository maintained by Bitcoin Core. Current moderators are Ruben Somsen, Bryan Bishop, and Warren Togami.

Participation is open to anyone, but primarily targets contributors with technical expertise in Bitcoin. If you'd like to receive these discussions by e-mail, you can subscribe to this mailing list. If you'd rather have a summary of these discussions, you can also subscribe to the Bitcoin Optech newsletter: https://bitcoinops.org/en/newsletters/
