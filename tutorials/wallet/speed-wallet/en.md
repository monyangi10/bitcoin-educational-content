## Getting started with Speed Wallet


Speed Wallet is a multi-currency custodial wallet supporting transactions on the Bitcoin main network, Lightning network transactions and also USDT (Tether USD) stable currency transactions. In this tutorial, we will focus exclusively on the use of Speed Wallet on the Bitcoin networks.


Speed Wallet is available as a mobile application on the Android (Google Play Store) and iOS (Apple Store) platforms, but also as a Chrome extension that lets you carry out transactions from your computer.


You can find links to download platforms on the official [Speed Wallet] website (https://speed.app).


⚠️ **IMPORTANT**: It is essential to download your wallets from official download platforms to guarantee the authenticity of the application.


In this tutorial, we'll be focusing on the app's Android platform, but all the processes that will be presented in this tutorial are equally valid on iOS.


Speed Wallet requires the creation of a user account. You can create one from your Google account, or define a strong email and password.

### Making your first transaction with Speed Wallet


If you already have experience with Bitcoin wallets, you won't be out of step at all. Speed Wallet offers you an intuitive Interface to help you get started.


On the **Wallet** home page, you'll find :



- A section for the currency with which you wish to operate.



- Your balance in the chosen currency.



- An option to create and personalize your Lightning address to receive Lightning payments.


![lightning-address](assets/fr/03.webp)


⚠️ **NOTE**: A Lightning address is a unique email alias that simplifies the reading of your Lightning URL. Be sure to provide the correct Lightning address when you wish to receive Lightning payments.



- Receiving payments on Speed Wallet:


Click on the **Receive** button, then select the layer on which you wish to receive and the transaction amount to share your invoice with your sender.


You can also simply use your Lightning address when you want to give your sender the flexibility to define the transaction amount.


![receive-bitcoins](assets/fr/04.webp)



- Send bitcoins with **Speed Wallet**:


From the **Send** button, you can send bitcoins using your recipient's Lightning address, Lightning invoice or Bitcoin (Mainnet) address.


Speed Wallet will detect, based on your address format, the network on which it should operate, giving you a smoother experience.


![send-btc](assets/fr/05.webp)

## Earn rewards


In addition to the built-in applications, Speed Wallet implements an evolutionary process based on your use of the wallet. Depending on the regularity of your transactions, you'll earn Speed tokens that make you eligible for a reward that you can receive in bitcoins.


Speed Wallet also features an invitation system that lets you earn bitcoins every time someone uses your link to create their Speed Wallet wallet.


To qualify, you must meet the following criteria:



- The sponsorship program is available in your country.



- Your account is at least 24 Hours old.



- You have at least **1000 Satoshis or 1 USDT** in your wallet.



- Invitation and rewards are not restricted to your account.

## Speed Wallet for retail


Speed Wallet places particular emphasis on the integration and use of Bitcoin and stablecoins in businesses around the world.


From [Speed Business](https://www.tryspeed.com/), you have a unified payment aggregator to accept Bitcoin, powered by Lightning Network that you can use in your store, online or physical.


Essentially focused on payments, you will find the following options:



- **Online payments**: With this option, you can accept Bitcoin as a means of payment for your digital products, via payment links, invoicing and subscriptions.



- **On-site payments**: To collect payments in your store.



- **Instant payments**: An option that lets you manage reimbursements, withdrawals, expenses and employee pay slips directly from Speed Business Interface.



- **Platform payments**: Connect your Speed Business account to external tools you use to make transfers and payments to these platforms.
