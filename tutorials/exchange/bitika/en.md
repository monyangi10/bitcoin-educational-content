Wealth creation and regional and community integration are based on economic exchanges. These exchanges encourage the circulation of money and stimulate consumption. Over time, economic exchanges have taken many different forms, from bartering to the digital economy and the industrial economy.


Africa is increasingly becoming a breeding ground for the digital economy, particularly through :


- mobile money transfer services (the first service of this type, M-Pesa, was launched in Kenya by telephone operator Safaricom);
- and digital assets (mainly Bitcoin for its decentralized, uncensored nature, etc.).


*software engineer *FIDEL OTIENO** then created a solution - **Bitika** - that combines Bitcoin and mobile money to give people easier access to financial services.


The **Bitika** solution is a Kenyan platform designed not to impose new tools on day-to-day transactions, but to accompany the local population towards Bitcoin via M-Pesa, to which they have been accustomed for years.


Kenyans can therefore buy satoshis in three simple steps, without KYC or complex exchange platforms.


## Getting started with Bitika


To use the solution, visit the [Bitika] platform (https://bitika.xyz/). Whether on your computer or smartphone, the interface remains the same.


![screen|500](assets/fr/03.webp)


![screen|250](assets/fr/04.webp)


You have :

1. the field for entering the amount ;

2. the field for pasting your Lightning address (addresses only) ;

3. gW-3 price in real time in dollars ;

4. recommended Lightning wallets, if you don't already have one.


## The three stages of purchasing


![screen|500](assets/fr/05.webp)


To make your purchase :

1. enter the amount of your purchase (in **KES**) and the Lightning address of your wallet;

2. enter and confirm your M-Pesa number;

3. enter the PIN code of your M-Pesa mobile money account to confirm your purchase.


Your wallet is almost instantly credited with your satoshis.


![screen|250](assets/fr/06.webp)


Please note that the amount of your purchase on **Bitika** cannot be **less than** **50 KES** nor **more than** **10,000 KES** (Kenyan shillings). The platform has already experienced a shortage of satoshis (*out of stock*) due to high demand. This demonstrates the enthusiasm of the local population for the solution.


Remember to check the details carefully and tick the box before initiating payment. Also, service charges apply.


![screen|250](assets/fr/07.webp)


In addition, **OTP verification** was added a few months ago to secure transactions due to scammers posing as platforms and enticing people to send money via M-Pesa. A one-time code is then sent to your phone by SMS, valid for a short time. You then enter it in the field provided. Once validated, you will be redirected to the payment page.
