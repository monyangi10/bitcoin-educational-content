The adoption of Bitcoin has become massive all around the globe, thanks to the possibilities offered by Lightning Network. Several solutions now exist to enable you to be part of this wave of adoption, and integrate Bitcoin as a means of payment to give your service, your business, a global reach to receive instant payments from anywhere in the world. In this approach, Flash, a self-custody solution, stands out for its functionality and user experience.


## Getting started with Flash


Flash is a platform that enables you, as a merchant or entrepreneur, to collect payments based on the Lightning overlay of the Bitcoin protocol. The platform is fairly intuitive, and right from the start it takes you through the steps involved in setting up your organization.


### Setting up your organization


Take advantage of Flash's simplicity to set up your organization in just three steps.


- Fill in the details of your organization:

The Flash platform accepts businesses from all continents, including Africa (Nigeria, Ghana), Europe, the Americas and Asia (Vietnam, China).

- No identification is required on the platform. Simply enter your first and last names, then the country from which you wish to operate, then continue the process.

Once you've connected your Bitcoin wallet, specify the features that interest you and that you'd like to use on the platform. These include

### Create a point of sale


Do you already have a physical store in your area and would like to accept Bitcoin payments? Flash's **Point of Sale** option is the right solution for you.


When you create your point of sale, you'll be given a web link which is the Interface you'll use to carry out actions in the point of sale.


![pos-link](assets/fr/08.webp)



On your dashboard page, click on **Start** in the **Point of Sale** quadrant. You will be redirected to the Interface for configuring your point of sale. Fill in the information corresponding to your point of sale and associate it with the portfolio you previously connected, then submit your data.


![pdv](assets/fr/09.webp)



Before you go to this Interface to start collecting payments in Bitcoin, we recommend that you create available products in your store for better traceability, in order to have an inventory on Flash.


On the dashboard, click on the **More** icon, then click on **New product**.


![products](assets/fr/10.webp)


Fill in your product information and link it to your point of sale.


![link_product](assets/fr/11.webp)


You can access your Point of Sale menu by clicking on the name of your point of sale in the **Active services** section.


![active_service](assets/fr/12.webp)


Copy the Interface link of your point of sale and access it. In the **Products** section, you'll find a list of all the products you've associated with this outlet. You can then generate Bitcoin (Lightning) invoices to charge your customers.


![pos1](assets/fr/13.webp)


### Payment links


Flash allows you to accept payments from anywhere in the world via Bitcoin not only via a point-of-sale, but also via a payment link service. You can generate payment links as a freelancer, project developer or whatever your status. These links are easy to share with your recipient and enable you to receive bitcoins directly into your self-custody wallet, without any intermediaries.


On the Flash dashboard, click on **Payment link** and fill in the form to generate your payment link.

### Create your store


Are you a new merchant, or don't have a website on which to integrate Flash? The platform offers you a site-building option that lets you create Flash stores and sell your products while accepting payments in Bitcoin.


In the menu, go to Interface for the **Shop** option, then click on **New shop**.


Configure the details of your store by entering a unique name visible only to you, a public name for your visitors, your store description and your brand identity visuals (logo, cover poster). Select the currency in which you wish to set up the products on your website. The prices of these products will be converted into Bitcoin using the current market conversion rate, and connect the Bitcoin wallet on which you wish to receive payments.
