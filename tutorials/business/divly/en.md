## Introduction

This tutorial explains how to use [Divly](https://divly.com/) to prepare a Bitcoin (BTC) tax report. Preparing a tax report involves gathering all relevant BTC transactions in a financial year, calculating taxable gains or income, and exporting a report that can be submitted to your local tax authority.

In jurisdictions where Bitcoin is subject to tax rules, you must report profits or losses from BTC disposals and income received in BTC. Using a tax reporting software helps you organize this information and generate a tax report efficiently.

## Getting Started

To begin:

- Create a Divly account.
- Set your **country of tax residence** and **local currency**.
- Confirm that these settings reflect the jurisdiction where you report taxes.

Divly uses this configuration to apply appropriate tax rules and currency conversions when generating your report.

![img](assets/en/01.webp)

## Step 1 - Import All Bitcoin Transactions from your wallets and exchanges

All Bitcoin transactions for the tax year must be imported before any tax calculations are made.

Divly supports multiple import methods:

- **API or OAuth connections** for exchanges or services that support it
- **CSV file uploads** from wallets or exchanges
- **Manual entry** for any transactions not covered by automated methods

Ensure you import **every BTC inflow and outflow** relevant to the tax period you are reporting.

![img](assets/en/02.webp)

## Step 2 - Review Imported Transactions

**Confirm your crypto balance:**

The best place to start is by checking that the total crypto you own matches the number displayed in Divly. Divly calculates your holdings by summing up all the transactions you have imported.

To do this, start by navigating to the Overview page. Check that each individual crypto listed is indeed the number you own. Divly does not display your fiat currencies in the Overview, so please ignore them in this exercise.

You can filter by wallet if you find any issues. This helps you understand what wallet may be out of synch.

![img](assets/en/03.webp)

After importing:

- Go to the **Transactions** section.
- Check that every transaction appears with correct dates and amounts.
- Resolve missing price or cost basis warnings where necessary.

**Important:** Make sure to import **ALL** of your crypto transactions into Divly before you proceed to the next step. Including cold wallets! Otherwise there is a risk your taxes won't be correct.

![img](assets/en/04.webp)

## Step 3 - Categorize relevant deposits and withdrawals

Different types of crypto transactions can have distinct tax implications. These include activities such as gifting cryptocurrency, lost assets, mining rewards, forks, airdrops, and similar events. It is important that all transactions are categorized accurately.

In most cases, Divly automatically assigns the correct labels. However, when the available transaction data is insufficient, automatic classification may not be possible. In these situations, it is the user’s responsibility to manually assign the appropriate label. To understand the meaning and tax treatment of each label, please refer to the related help article.

To label transactions, go to the Transactions page. Select one or more transactions and choose the correct label from the dropdown menu at the top of the page.

![img](assets/en/05.webp)

## Step 4 - Generate the Tax Report

Once transactions are imported and classified:

- Go to the **Tax Report** section.
- Select the relevant **tax year**.
- Review the summary of calculated gains, losses, and income categories.

The summary aggregates taxable events based on the imported data and classifications.

Divly’s tax report interface allows you to confirm that all transactions are captured before exporting.

![img](assets/en/06.webp)

## Step 5 - Export the Report

After review:

- Export the finalized BTC tax report in the format available for your country.
- Save the exported file or print it for submission to your tax authority.

![img](assets/en/07.webp)

Depending on your jurisdiction, you may need to follow specific submission instructions or use country-specific forms with your exported data. [Divly’s country-specific guides](https://divly.com/en/guides) can help you with submission steps if needed.
